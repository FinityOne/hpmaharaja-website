## Introduction

The world is ever-evolving, and with this change comes a change in humans — their behavior, perspective, and most importantly, their action patterns. Karma is a pattern, and whatever action is taken, an equal but opposite reaction is applied. The crazier the action, the crazier the result is.

With today's generation showing lower and lower attention spans, the world has become even more unpredictable when it comes to human behavior. This article takes a deeper look at the intricacies of the modern world, and how it compares to the old world — a comparison that is essential for analyzing the evolution of human living quality and societal outcomes.

---

## Key Areas of Disconnect

The evolution of society has led to significant disconnects between the old and modern worlds. These differences reveal themselves across several key areas:

- **Money aspects:** The shift from value-based trade to profit-driven economies.
- **Family & Social Network:** Changes in human-to-human connections and community reliance.
- **New connection pitfalls:** Digital interactions replacing in-person bonds.
- **Cultural and environmental factors:** Erosion of tradition and environmental consequences.
- **Spirituality & Higher Connection:** A diminishing role of spirituality in daily life.

Before diving into the specifics, it's important to establish a "gold standard" — a guiding principle for what humans should aim to achieve on this planet: genuine happiness and harmony.

---

## Money: A Shift in Purpose

The way money moved in the old world versus how it operates today has a direct effect on our lives.

In the **old world**, money was a means, not an end.  
Its role was simple:

- Facilitate trade  
- Represent value  
- Create balance between goods, services, and skill  
- Encourage people to master their craft  
- Maintain clarity through defined roles  

Money being backed by limited resources (like gold) created a natural restraint that supported stability, service, and family unity.

In the **modern world**, the purpose has flipped:

- Money is now the *goal*  
- Short-term gains outweigh long-term value  
- Social media creates false expectations  
- People chase shortcuts instead of skills  
- Gender roles blur in destructive ways  
- Families and relationships destabilize under financial confusion  

People today are drowning in the illusion of ownership. The society-wide chase for more money — disconnected from purpose — has created internal emptiness and external chaos.

## Human-to-Human Connection: Family & Social Network

This is perhaps the saddest downfall of the modern world.

In the **old world**:

- People talked face-to-face  
- Bonds were real, deep, and emotional  
- Loyalty and integrity mattered  
- Time was abundant and freely given  
- Communities relied on each other  
- Social harmony was a natural byproduct  

Today:

- Everyone claims to be "busy" doing nothing  
- Attention spans are destroyed  
- Social media replaces real human bonding  
- People are programmed to seek fake, one-sided interactions  
- Dating becomes harder due to low patience, low tolerance, low commitment  
- Hyper-independence kills family formation  
- Mindsets become self-centered and transactional  

Society is becoming a collection of isolated individuals living inside digital illusions.

---

## New Connection Pitfalls

Technology is powerful — but also dangerous.

Modern humans are drowning in:

- Notifications  
- Dopamine loops  
- Digital validation  
- Emotional detachment  
- Hyper-individualism  

Tech creators are motivated by profit, not humanity. As a result:

- Tech increases convenience  
- But decreases fulfillment  
- People become less grounded  
- And increasingly disconnected from their heart  

We crave human emotion, but drown in digital interaction.

## Environmental Concerns

Your environment shapes your life.

In the old world:

- Communities were selective  
- “Bad apples” were removed  
- Standards were high  
- People were loyal  
- Trust was sacred  
- Environments were built for growth  

Today:

- Rapid cultural blending creates confusion  
- Standards drop  
- People accept anything  
- Boundaries vanish  
- Priorities blur  
- Negative influences spread faster than positive ones  

What should uplift society now often tears it apart.

## Spirituality

In the old world:

- Spirituality aligned society  
- Actions matched beliefs  
- People respected higher power
